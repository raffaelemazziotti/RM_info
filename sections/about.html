About

My career began in Experimental Psychology at the University of Florence, where I worked on a behavioural tasks to measure how mice recognise and remember visual scenes. This curiosity about the maturing visual system led me to a PhD in Basic and Developmental Neuroscience at the University of Pisa, where I focused on the microRNA miR‑132 , whose expression is shaped by visual experience; we found it is indispensable for normal depth perception. In parallel, I built a low‑cost optical‑imaging platform that turns rapid changes in cortical blood flow into clear read‑outs of brain activity. Using this tool in a mouse model of CDKL5 Deficiency Disorder, we uncovered weakened visual responses and trained an AI system that can quantify disease severity and track the effects of emerging therapies. Recognising the power of advanced analytics, I completed an international master’s programme in Data Science, developing a cross‑species deep‑learning model to analyse subtle shifts in pupil dynamics and reveal how sensory information is processed in both animals and humans. Today I combine behaviour, electrophysiology, and functional near‑infrared spectroscopy to investigate neurovascular signatures of autism and other neurodevelopmental disorders in laboratory models and patients. My goal remains constant: to merge experimental psychology with the tools provided by neurobiology, transforming foundational insights into biomarkers that can guide new treatments for neurodevelopmental disorders.
